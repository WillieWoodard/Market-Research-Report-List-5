<p><p>Endotracheal suctioning devices are used to remove secretions from the airway. Closed system devices have a catheter enclosed in a sealed container, reducing the risk of contamination and infection. Open system devices have a catheter connected to a suction source without a container, allowing for easier access but posing a higher risk of contamination. Both types of devices are important for maintaining airway patency in patients who require mechanical ventilation or are unable to clear their airways effectively.</p></p>
