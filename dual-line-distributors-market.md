<p><p>In a one-way output market, dual line distributors receive products from manufacturers and sell them to retailers or end consumers, with no possibility for the products to be returned to the manufacturer. In contrast, in a two-way output market, dual line distributors can return unsold products back to the manufacturer, reducing the risk of excess inventory. One-way output markets are more common in industries with stable demand, while two-way output markets are more common in industries with fluctuating demand.</p></p>
