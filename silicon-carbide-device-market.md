<p><p>Silicon Carbide Devices are categorized into three main markets based on their voltage ratings: High Voltage Devices, Medium Voltage Devices, and Low Voltage Devices. High Voltage Devices are designed to operate at voltages greater than 1,700 volts, making them suitable for power distribution and grid applications. Medium Voltage Devices typically operate at voltages between 1,200 and 1,700 volts, catering to industrial and renewable energy applications. Low Voltage Devices operate at voltages below 1,200 volts and are used in consumer electronics and automotive applications.</p></p>
