<p><p>Graphene fibers can be categorized into three types: Polymer Combined Fibers, Inorganic Metals Combined Fibers, and Inorganic Nonmetals Combined Fibers. Polymer Combined Fibers are produced by combining graphene with various polymers to enhance their mechanical and electrical properties. Inorganic Metals Combined Fibers involve incorporating graphene with metals to create high strength and conductive materials. Inorganic Nonmetals Combined Fibers consist of graphene combined with non-metallic materials to achieve properties such as flexibility and thermal conductivity. Each type caters to different applications and industries.</p></p>
