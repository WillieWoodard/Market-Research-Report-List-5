<p><p>Synthetic Glycerin can be categorized into two main types - Type I and Type II. Type I synthetic glycerin refers to glycerin produced from petrochemical sources, such as propylene or acrolein. This type is commonly used in industrial applications, such as the production of resins, paints, and plastics. On the other hand, Type II synthetic glycerin is derived from bio-based feedstocks, like vegetable oils or animal fats. This type is often used in personal care products, pharmaceuticals, and food products due to its natural origin and environmentally friendly properties.</p></p>
