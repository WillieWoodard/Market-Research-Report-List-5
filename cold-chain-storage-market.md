<p><p>The Cold Chain Storage market comprises of two main types: Chilled and Frozen. Chilled storage is maintained at temperatures above freezing, typically between 0 to 4 degrees Celsius, to prolong the freshness and quality of perishable products such as fruits, vegetables, and dairy products. Frozen storage, on the other hand, is kept at temperatures below freezing, usually between -18 to -24 degrees Celsius, to preserve the quality of frozen foods like meat, seafood, and ice cream. Both types are crucial in ensuring the safe transportation and storage of perishable goods.</p></p>
