<p><ul><li>Whole Tea Leaves</li><li>Broken Leaves</li><li>Fannings</li><li>Dust</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Darjeeling tea is categorised into four main market types based on the size of the tea leaves. Whole tea leaves are considered the highest grade, offering a full-bodied flavor and aroma. Broken leaves are slightly smaller and are known for their strong taste. Fannings are even smaller in size and are commonly used in tea bags for a quick brew. Dust is the smallest grade, often used in commercial tea bags for a fast infusion but sacrificing some flavor complexity compared to whole or broken leaves.</p></p>
